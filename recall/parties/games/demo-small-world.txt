[SHARED RULES]
PREMISE:
Small-world topology demo. Two clusters connected by a single bridge.
Cluster 1: Hub-1 + 3 members. Cluster 2: Hub-2 + 3 members.
Hubs are connected to each other (the bridge). Members only talk within their cluster.
Information must cross the bridge to reach the other side.

MECHANICS:
- clusters: Number of clusters (2).
- bridges: Number of inter-cluster links (1, between hubs).
- diameter: Maximum hops between any two agents (3).
- status: Each agent's state (local, bridging, done).

RULES:
- Each cluster discusses internally. Members DM their hub. Hubs coordinate members.
- Cross-cluster communication ONLY goes through the bridge: Hub-1 ↔ Hub-2.
- Cluster members CANNOT talk to anyone outside their cluster. Only their hub.
- Hubs relay information between clusters as needed.
- Task: each cluster generates a piece of information. Hubs exchange via bridge. Both clusters must end up with the combined knowledge.
- Keep messages to 1-2 sentences. Demonstrate the bottleneck.
