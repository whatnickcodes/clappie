# Evidence Quality Rubric

## Evidence Hierarchy

| Level | Description | Study Types | Confidence |
|-------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| **A** | High-quality evidence | Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, large RCTs (n>200), well-designed multi-center trials | Strong |
| **B** | Moderate evidence | Smaller RCTs (n<200), well-designed prospective cohort studies, case-control with clear methodology | Moderate |
| **C** | Low-quality evidence | Retrospective studies, case series (n>50), registry data, post-hoc analyses | Limited |
| **D** | Very low evidence | Case reports, expert opinion, consensus statements without formal evidence review, animal/in-vitro studies | Weak |

## GRADE Mapping

Map evidence levels to GRADE recommendation strength:

| GRADE | Meaning | Typical Evidence | Phrasing |
|-------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Strong FOR** | Benefits clearly outweigh risks | Level A-B, consistent results | "We recommend..." |
| **Conditional FOR** | Benefits probably outweigh risks | Level B-C, or A with inconsistency | "We suggest..." |
| **Conditional AGAINST** | Risks probably outweigh benefits | Level B-C against | "We suggest against..." |
| **Strong AGAINST** | Risks clearly outweigh benefits | Level A-B against, consistent results | "We recommend against..." |

## Applying the Rubric

### When Curating Evidence (Step 3)

For each included article, assign:
1. **Evidence level** (A/B/C/D) based on study design
2. **Risk of bias** (low/moderate/high) — consider: randomization, blinding, attrition, selective reporting
3. **Applicability** (direct/indirect) — does the population match the presentation audience?

### Evidence Table Format

```
| # | Citation | Design | N | Level | Key Finding | Bias | Notes |
|---|----------|--------|---|-------|-------------|------|-------|
```

### Handling Conflicting Evidence

When studies at the same level disagree:
1. **Report both positions** with citation counts
2. **Note the conflict** explicitly: "Evidence is conflicting at Level B..."
3. **Assess heterogeneity sources**: population differences, intervention dosing, outcome definitions
4. **Default to the larger/newer study** when quality is equivalent
5. **Flag for user review** in Step 3 checkpoint

### Minimum Evidence Requirements by Presentation Type

| Type | Minimum Level A/B | Minimum Total Sources |
|------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Lecture | 3 | 15-25 |
| Debate | 2 per position | 10-20 |
| Case Presentation | 1 | 8-15 |
| Literature Review | 5 | 20-40 |

### Downgrading and Upgrading

**Downgrade** evidence level when:
- High risk of bias in the study design
- Serious inconsistency across studies
- Indirectness (population mismatch)
- Imprecision (wide confidence intervals)

**Upgrade** evidence level when:
- Large effect size (RR >2 or <0.5)
- Dose-response gradient present
- All plausible confounders would reduce the effect
